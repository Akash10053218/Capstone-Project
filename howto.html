How to Play

Learn the basics of DO or DIE! and start your journey to survival or deception.

1

Join or Create a Room

Start by joining an existing game room or create your own. You can invite friends using a room code or join public matches. Wait for enough players to join before the game begins (typically 4-10 players).

2

Receive Your Secret Role

Once the game starts, you'll be randomly assigned a role: Survivor, Saboteur, or Detective. Your role is secret - only you know what you are. Your objectives depend on your role, so pay attention to the instructions.

3

Complete Tasks or Sabotage

If you're a Survivor or Detective, complete tasks around the map to progress the mission. If you're a Saboteur, sabotage systems, eliminate players, and prevent task completion. Use your abilities wisely and avoid suspicion.

4

Report and Discuss

When a body is found or a sabotage occurs, an emergency meeting is called. Use this time to discuss what happened, share information, and try to identify the Saboteurs. Be convincing in your defense or accusations.

5

Vote Out Suspicious Players

After discussion, vote to eliminate the most suspicious player. If you're a Saboteur, try to frame innocent players. If you're a Survivor, work together to identify the threats. The player with the most votes is eliminated.

6

Survive Till the End

The game continues until one team achieves their win condition. Survivors win by completing all tasks or eliminating all Saboteurs. Saboteurs win by equalizing numbers or preventing task completion. Play strategically and adapt to the situation!

Pro Tips

  • Watch Behavior: Pay attention to how players move and act - Saboteurs often behave differently
  • Use Alibis: Always have a story ready about where you were and what you were doing
  • Stick Together: Survivors should travel in groups to avoid being eliminated
  • Blend In: Saboteurs should act like Survivors and complete fake tasks to avoid suspicion
  • Take Notes: Remember who was where and when - information is your best weapon
  • Trust No One: Even if someone seems trustworthy, they might be deceiving you
